# CONTROLLED PULSE - Season 1, Chapter 1

## 🌧️ **Rescue in the Storm**

The night was pitch black, rain cascading down as if the sky itself had been torn apart.

Hong Kong Central Hospital's neon lights reflected on the flooded pavement, shattering into countless fragments of starlight. Lin Muqing stood at the hospital entrance, gazing at the torrential rain, and sighed.

"Again," she muttered to herself, adjusting the collar of her white coat and picking up her handbag.

Three years as a cardiac surgeon, Muqing had already grown accustomed to this kind of life. Saving lives, protecting health, but also facing endless pressure and challenges. She had performed eight surgeries today, including three emergency open-heart procedures—each a race against death.

She opened her umbrella and stepped into the rain. Her high heels clicked on the slippery sidewalk, each step requiring special care. Muqing glanced at her watch—it was already 2 AM. The last bus should have left long ago.

"Looks like I'll need to take a taxi," she said, pulling out her phone. The ride-sharing app displayed "No cars available."

With no other choice, Muqing decided to walk to the nearest subway station. Though farther, at least she'd have some cover.

Raindrops pounded on the umbrella surface, creating a muffled drumbeat. Muqing quickened her pace, her heels creating crisp sounds that were mostly drowned out by the rain.

As she passed a narrow, deserted alley, she heard—

A muffled groan.

Not thunder.

Not wind.

A human voice.

A man's suppressed cry of pain, pushed to the brink.

Muqing's heart tightened, her footsteps freezing.

"Is someone there?" she called out, but her voice was swallowed by the rain.

The alley entrance was only deep darkness, rain curtains isolating the outside world. She hesitated for a moment, her medical instincts taking over.

If someone was injured, she couldn't just leave them to die.

She put away her umbrella and stepped into the alley. Her phone flashlight illuminated the path, the beam appearing particularly weak in the rain.

The alley was darker, the graffiti on the walls washed blurry by the rain. The air was filled with the mixed smell of garbage and rainwater.

Suddenly, the beam of light fell on a figure slumped against the wall—

The man was covered in blood, his left shoulder deep enough to expose bone. Rain mixed with crimson, spreading in red streaks across the pooled water.

He looked up, his eyes like the deep sea, yet fixed firmly on Muqing.

"Don't... come..."

His voice was hoarse, filled with extreme alertness.

Muqing's heart was pricked, but medical training took over. She tossed aside her umbrella and knelt beside the man.

"You're losing too much blood. This isn't the time to be stubborn!"

The man's breathing was rapid, his chest heaving as if he might faint at any moment.

His white shirt was soaked through with blood, the wound on his left shoulder horrifying. Muqing quickly examined the injury, discovering abnormal tissue changes around the edge—not like an ordinary knife wound.

"My name is Lin Muqing. I'm a cardiac surgeon. I'll help you." Muqing maintained her composure, but the man's abnormal reactions made her uneasy.

The man suddenly grabbed her wrist—his grip was surprisingly strong.

"Listen..." he leaned close to Muqing's ear, his voice so weak it was almost drowned by the downpour:

"If anyone asks... say you never saw me..."

Before he could finish, the man had already lost consciousness.

Muqing froze, immediately checking his vital signs. His pulse was weak, his blood pressure low—he needed immediate hospital transport.

But the warning in his eyes flashed through her mind—not fear, but something deeper.

Pretense? Threat? Or... the truth?

The rain still poured relentlessly. Muqing knew tonight was only the beginning.

## 🎭 **Character Psychological Portrayal**

Lin Muqing's heart was a tangled mess of emotions.

As a doctor, her first instinct was to save lives. But this man's abnormal behavior put her on alert.

She recalled today's final surgery—a heart transplant patient who suddenly showed rejection symptoms. Though ultimately saved, the process had been terrifying.

At the same time, she remembered last week's argument with the hospital board. About a new heart technology research project. She had worried about ethical issues, but the administration insisted on proceeding for profit.

Could this man's injury be related to the hospital's research?

Muqing shook her head, thinking herself too suspicious. But as a doctor, she knew sometimes intuition was the best warning system.

She quickly decided: transport to hospital first, then investigate.

## 📚 Chapter 2: Night Shift at the Hospital
# CONTROLLED PULSE - Season 1, Chapter 2

## 🏥 **Night Shift at the Hospital**

"Quick! To the emergency room!"

As Lin Muqing helped lift the man onto a stretcher, her phone rang. It was Chen Meiling, the night shift head nurse.

"Muqing, where are you? The ER is short-staffed."

"I'm bringing in an injured patient. The situation is quite urgent." Muqing explained briefly.

"I understand. I'll arrange it," Chen Meiling heard the seriousness in Muqing's tone and immediately understood this wasn't a simple case.

In the emergency room, the medical team was already prepared. Muqing quickly assessed the man's vital signs.

"Blood pressure 80/50, pulse 120, rapid breathing. The left shoulder wound needs immediate suturing, but first we need to rule out internal bleeding." Muqing directed, "Prepare blood transfusion, get a CT scan."

The anesthesiologist approached: "Do we need anesthesia?"

"He's consciousness is unstable. Let's hold off on anesthetic drugs for now." Muqing observed the man's reactions carefully, "I suspect the wound may involve nerves. We need to be careful during treatment."

After the CT scan results came out, Muqing's expression grew grim.

"Why would it be like this?" she muttered to herself.

Chen Meiling came over: "What's wrong, Muqing?"

"The wound on his left shoulder isn't a simple knife injury." Muqing pointed at the image on the screen, "Look at this position. It affects the triceps, but the tissue changes... something's off."

"What's off about it?"

"Too perfect, too regular." Muqing shook her head, "It doesn't look like an accident. More like... it was designed."

Designed injuries?

Chen Meiling froze: "You mean..."

"I'm not sure, but his injury is abnormal." Muqing looked at the unconscious man, "Let's treat the wound first, we'll deal with the rest later."

The surgery proceeded remarkably smoothly. Muqing's technique was exquisite, every cut and suture precise. But there was always a question in her mind.

Why such perfectly designed injuries?

The man suddenly let out a soft groan.

"He's waking up?" the anesthesiologist asked concernedly.

"It's fine. He should pass out again." Muqing continued suturing, "But remember, when he wakes up, absolutely no anesthetic drugs."

"Why?"

"I suspect he may have allergic reactions to certain medications." Muqing explained, "It's best to wait until he's fully conscious before further treatment."

Three hours later, the man finally regained full consciousness.

He opened his eyes, the first thing he saw was the bright lights of the hospital ceiling. Then he saw Lin Muqing standing by his bed.

"You..." The man tried to sit up, but the pain made him fall back onto the bed.

"Don't move around. The wound isn't fully healed yet." Muqing helped him lie back down, "My name is Lin Muqing. I'm the doctor who performed your surgery."

The man was silent for a moment, then spoke: "Thank you."

"No need. I'm a doctor. Saving lives is my duty." Muqing maintained professional distance, "But I need to know how you sustained such serious injuries."

The man's expression became guarded: "I fell."

Muqing almost laughed: "Falling would cause a shoulder wound exposing bone?"

"I don't want to talk about it." The man turned his head away, "I'll pay the medical fees."

"The medical fees aren't the issue." Muqing sat down, "But as your doctor, I need to know your injury status to ensure your full recovery."

The man looked at her, his expression complex. "You're not an ordinary doctor."

"I'm a cardiac surgeon. Professional medical care is my responsibility." Muqing's tone became serious, "If you're withholding information, it could affect your treatment effectiveness."

Silence.

Then the man sighed softly: "My name is Zhou Zixuan."

"Mr. Zhou, your injury is somewhat unusual." Muqing got to the point, "I suspect it wasn't an accidental injury."

Zhou Zixuan suddenly became tense: "What are you talking about?"

"The location and depth of your left shoulder wound are too perfect." Muqing explained, "It's as if someone specifically designed it to injure a specific area."

Zhou Zixuan's expression became unreadable: "You... how would you know?"

"I'm a doctor. I've seen many injury cases." Muqing remained calm, "But I've never seen injuries like this before."

Zhou Zixuan was silent for a long time, then spoke softly: "You wouldn't understand."

"I understand medicine, I understand human anatomy." Muqing looked directly into his eyes, "If you trust me, you should tell me the truth."

Zhou Zixuan looked at Muqing, his eyes filled with struggle. Finally, he gently shook his head: "I'm sorry, Dr. Lin. I can't tell you anything."

"But..."

"I'll pay the full medical fees, then leave." Zhou Zixuan turned his head away, "Don't ask anymore."

Muqing knew that tonight she had only touched the tip of the iceberg.
